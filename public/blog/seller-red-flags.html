7 Seller Behaviors That Scream 'Walk Away' from a Used Car Deal

By Manny T. · May 22, 2026 · 8 min read

After years of buying cars, you start to notice patterns. Certain things sellers say or do are almost always red flags. Here are the ones that should make you walk away immediately.

1. Refuses to give you the VIN upfront

Legitimate sellers have nothing to hide. If they won't give you the VIN before you drive to see the car, they're hiding something. Run the VIN before you waste your time.

2. Pushes to meet in a parking lot instead of at their home

Curbstoners and people flipping problem cars almost always want a neutral location. They don't want you seeing where the car actually lives or that there are multiple cars for sale from the same spot.

3. Vague or changing stories about why they're selling

"Just upgrading" is fine. "My wife doesn't like it" is fine. "It just sits in the garage" or "I bought it for my kid but they changed their mind" often means there's a problem they're not disclosing.

4. Pressure to buy today or "someone else is coming tomorrow"

This is a classic high-pressure tactic. Good deals don't disappear overnight. If they're rushing you, they're hiding something or the car isn't worth what they're asking.

5. Won't let you take it to a mechanic or do a thorough inspection

Any seller who won't let you get a pre-purchase inspection is hiding major issues. This is non-negotiable on anything over a few thousand dollars.

6. The title is not in their name or they "have to get it from their brother"

This is a huge red flag for title washing, liens, or the car not actually belonging to the person selling it. Walk away.

7. Cash only, no paperwork, "trust me bro"

Never pay cash for a car over a couple thousand dollars without proper paperwork and a traceable payment method. If they won't do it right, there's a reason.

Trust your gut. If something feels off, it usually is. There are plenty of good cars out there — don't get emotionally attached to a bad one.
