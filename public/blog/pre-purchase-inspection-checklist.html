The Ultimate Pre-Purchase Inspection Checklist for Used Cars

By Manny T. · May 25, 2026 · 12 min read

Before you hand over thousands of dollars, you need to know what you're actually buying. A thorough pre-purchase inspection can save you from expensive surprises.

Here is the exact checklist I used when buying cars for the dealership — adapted for private buyers.

Exterior Inspection

  • Check for uneven panel gaps and paint overspray (sign of accident repair).
  • Look at the tires — uneven wear can indicate alignment or suspension issues.
  • Inspect the undercarriage for rust, especially on frame rails and floor pans.
  • Check the trunk and spare tire area for water damage or signs of flooding.

Interior and Mechanical

  • Test every electrical function: windows, locks, A/C, radio, lights.
  • Check for water stains under carpets and seats.
  • Start the car cold and watch for smoke from the exhaust.
  • Listen for unusual noises from the engine, transmission, and suspension during a test drive.
  • Check the odometer against service records for consistency.

Paperwork and History

  • Verify the title is in the seller's name and is clear (no liens or brands).
  • Run a vehicle history report before you even go look at the car.
  • Ask for all service records and compare them to the mileage.
  • Check for open recalls on the NHTSA website using the VIN.

Bring a trusted mechanic if you're not comfortable doing this yourself. A few hundred dollars on an inspection can save you thousands later.
