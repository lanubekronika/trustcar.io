IN HIS OWN WORDS

I didn't start behind a desk. I started under cars.

As a technician, I learned how vehicles actually fail — not theory, but real-world patterns. Which engines burn oil by 60,000 miles. Which transmissions are already dying at 80k. Which "minor" repairs on a vehicle history report are actually early signs of something much more expensive.

When I moved into service advisory and management, I saw the other side of the counter. How reconditioning gets minimized before a car hits the lot. How sellers describe condition versus what we actually found on the lift. How a clean-looking car can have a file full of problems.

Now I work in extended car warranty. My job is to review mechanical claims and study 3rd-party inspection reports — then make the call: approved or denied. I see what breaks, when it breaks, and why. I coordinate with inspection companies, review their reports whether the inspection was done in person or virtually, and I decide based on what the report shows and what I know.

Most used car buyers never get access to this layer of knowledge. They get a vehicle history PDF and a seller's word.

That's not enough. That's why I built TrustCar.io.
